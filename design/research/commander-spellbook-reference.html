Commander / Spellbook Reference Research

Draft research note for Neon Champions tactical combat. This is reference synthesis, not an approved design spec.

Purpose

Compare how HoMM-like and adjacent tactics games handle hero/commander/spellbook powers, then extract lessons for Neon Champions’ Champion abilities, operations, and non-magic spellbook equivalent.

Cross-Reference Patterns

Pattern 1: Off-board commander + spellbook

Seen in HoMM 1-3, most HoMM titles, King’s Bounty, Songs of Conquest.

Strengths:

  • Keeps stacks/army composition central.
  • Commander feels powerful without occupying a tile.
  • Easy to cap cadence: one cast/operation per round, limited resource.
  • Fits current Neon decision that Champions are not battlefield units.

Risks:

  • Can feel like abstract magic if not grounded in setting.
  • If too powerful, commander powers dominate unit tactics.
  • If too weak, Champion identity fades.

Neon lesson:

Use Champion as off-board commander/interface. Powers should be Operations, Commands, network interventions, logistics calls, doctrine triggers, or Echo/Signal events rather than spells.

Pattern 2: Commander as battlefield unit

Seen in HoMM4, Age of Wonders, Planetfall, Shadowrun-style party tactics.

Strengths:

  • Very concrete and characterful.
  • Allows positional risk/reward and equipment builds.
  • Easy to understand in tactical RPGs.

Risks:

  • Blurs army-stack identity.
  • Adds pathing, survivability, targeting, death, and AI burden.
  • Conflicts with current Champion-as-commander direction.

Neon lesson:

Do not use for MVP. Champions should remain commander layer, not battlefield units.

Pattern 3: Once-per-round / cadence-limited powers

Seen strongly in HoMM-like spellcasting, including Olden Era’s accessible spell info.

Strengths:

  • Prevents spell/operation spam.
  • Creates meaningful timing choices.
  • Easy for AI and UI.

Risks:

  • If only one power per round, large spellbooks may feel underused.
  • Exceptions can become complex.

Neon lesson:

Default Champion Operation cadence should probably be one operation/command per round, with rare skill/upgrade exceptions.

Pattern 4: Army-generated casting resource

Strongly associated with Songs of Conquest’s Essence model.

Strengths:

  • Army composition directly shapes available powers.
  • Makes units matter beyond damage.
  • Encourages faction identity and mixed-army tradeoffs.

Risks:

  • Can become a second deck/resource puzzle.
  • Requires excellent UI forecasting.

Neon lesson:

Very promising. Neon equivalent could be Command, Bandwidth, Intel, Signal, Authority, or Operational Focus generated by stack types, logistics, Cohesion, territory, or Champion doctrine.

Pattern 5: Strategic + combat resource split

Seen in HoMM/King’s Bounty/AoW variants: persistent mana/economy plus combat-generated or cooldown-limited powers.

Strengths:

  • Strategic planning matters.
  • Combat tempo matters.
  • Supports powerful effects without unlimited spam.

Risks:

  • Too many currencies can confuse.

Neon lesson:

Consider two layers only:

  1. Strategic resource: Intel / Logistics / Network Access / Prepared Operations.
  2. Combat cadence/resource: once-per-round Champion action, possibly modified by Cohesion or generated Command/Bandwidth.

Recommended Direction for Neon Champions

Champion Role

Champions are off-board commanders, not battlefield units.

They affect battle through:

  • Commands — direct tactical orders, AP/initiative/Cohesion effects.
  • Operations — spellbook-equivalent tactical powers.
  • Doctrine — passive/conditional army-wide modifiers.
  • Logistics calls — reload, deploy, repair, resupply, extraction, drone drop.
  • Signal actions — spoof, jam, mark, sensor lock, counter-hack.
  • Echo/Continuity interventions — death/identity/afterimage effects where factionally appropriate.

Operation Cadence

Default proposal for later decision:

Possible exceptions:

  • Specialized Champion skill allows a second operation under constraints.
  • Some operations are passive/doctrine and do not consume the operation slot.
  • Some faction mechanics generate bonus minor commands, not full operations.

Resource Model Candidates

Avoid calling it mana. Candidate non-magic resources:

  • Command — broadest and easiest.
  • Bandwidth — good for network/Signal-heavy factions but too narrow globally.
  • Intel — already a Neon resource concept; may be too strategic and precious.
  • Operational Focus — clear but dry.
  • Authority — good for legitimacy/command but less tactical.
  • Access — good for network/logistics calls.

Potential split:

  • Strategic resource: Intel / Logistics / Access.
  • Combat limiter: Champion Operation slot + cooldown/charges.

Unit Abilities vs Champion Powers

Keep separate:

  • Unit abilities are stack-local: attack, defend, reload, overwatch, special attack, status, movement.
  • Champion Operations are army/battle-level: AP/initiative manipulation, area operation, supply call, Signal spoof, Cohesion restoration, tactical doctrine, emergency intervention.

Main Cautions

  • Do not copy HoMM magic literally; Neon has no direct magic.
  • Do not copy HoMM4/AoW hero-on-battlefield for MVP.
  • Do not overload with many currencies.
  • Do not let Champion powers dominate so much that unit rosters do not matter.
  • Do not make every operation Signal/network-based; analogue/low-tech factions need commander powers too.
